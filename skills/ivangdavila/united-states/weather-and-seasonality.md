# Weather and Seasonality — United States

## Macro Seasonal Strategy

| Season | Strong fits | Main risks |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Spring | Cities, desert shoulder trips, DC festivals | Rain volatility, spring break crowds |
| Summer | Mountain parks, Alaska, coastal escapes | Heat waves, hurricane season, wildfire smoke |
| Fall | Cities + foliage routes + parks | Early snow at elevation, shorter daylight |
| Winter | Sunbelt cities, ski destinations, Hawaii | Snow/ice disruptions, severe cold, road closures |

## Hurricane Window

- Atlantic and eastern Pacific hurricane season runs June 1 to November 30.
- Peak activity often concentrates late summer to early fall.
- Florida/Gulf/Atlantic trips should include cancellation-flex strategy.

## Heat and Air Quality

- Heat can become a trip-limiting hazard in desert and southern regions.
- Build indoor midday blocks during extreme heat windows.
- Check AQI via AirNow in wildfire-prone periods.

## Winter Risk

- Winter storms can affect roads and flights far beyond mountain states.
- Snow/ice travel requires larger transfer buffers and flexible bookings.
- Mountain drives in winter should include alternate routing options.

## Flood Risk

- Flash flooding is a major hazard during storm periods.
- Flooded roads can look passable but are high-risk; reroute early.
