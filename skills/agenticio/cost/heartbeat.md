# Cost Skill — Heartbeat

*Cost management is not a project. It is a discipline.*

---

## Weekly — Cost Pulse

"Here are any significant or unexpected costs from this week: [list]. Are there any patterns or concerns I should address?"

Weekly watch:
- Any invoices significantly above expectation
- Any new recurring charges that were not planned
- Labor costs vs hours budget if applicable
- Any vendor billing errors or duplicates

---

## Monthly — Full Cost Review

"Walk me through my monthly cost review. My total costs this month were [amount] versus [amount] last month and [amount] budget."

Monthly review covers:
1. Total costs vs budget by category
2. Cost of goods sold as percentage of revenue — is gross margin holding
3. Any cost categories with significant variance from plan
4. Vendor invoices reviewed for accuracy
5. Any contracts or subscriptions up for renewal

"Which cost categories are trending in the wrong direction and why?"

---

## Quarterly — Cost Structure Assessment

"It is end of quarter. Here is my cost summary: [breakdown]. Revenue was [amount]. Help me assess my cost structure and identify the biggest opportunities."

Quarterly assessment:
- Gross margin trend — three-quarter comparison
- Operating leverage — are costs growing slower than revenue
- Vendor spend review — any relationships worth renegotiating
- Headcount productivity — revenue or output per employee
- Software and subscription audit — anything unused or duplicative

---

## Annual — Cost Strategy Review

"Walk me through my annual cost review and planning for next year."

Annual checklist:
- Full vendor contract review — renewals, pricing, alternatives
- Insurance renewal review — coverage adequate, pricing competitive
- Facility costs — lease renewal considerations, space utilization
- Technology stack — consolidation opportunities, better alternatives
- Organizational structure — is the team configured efficiently for current scale
- Cost budget for next year — bottom-up rebuild rather than percentage increase on prior year

---

## Trigger Events — Use Immediately

Margin compression: "My gross margin dropped from [X]% to [Y]% this quarter. Help me diagnose the cause."

New hire decision: "I am considering hiring [role] at [salary]. Help me model the true cost and the revenue or efficiency justification."

Vendor price increase: "My vendor just informed me of a [X]% price increase. How do I respond and what are my options?"

Cost reduction mandate: "I need to cut [amount] from my cost base. Help me identify where to cut without damaging the business."

New product pricing: "I am launching a new product. Help me build the fully-loaded cost model so I can price it correctly."

Acquisition consideration: "I am looking at acquiring a business. Help me evaluate their cost structure and identify the risks."

---

*The businesses with the best cost structures are not the ones that cut the most. They are the ones that understand their costs well enough to spend deliberately and eliminate waste precisely.*
