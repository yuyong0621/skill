# Safety and Emergencies — United States

## Essential Numbers

- `911` for police, fire, and ambulance emergencies.
- `988` for mental health crisis support by call/text/chat.
- Poison Help: `1-800-222-1222` for exposure/ingestion incidents.

## Alert Systems to Use

- Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA) for local urgent notifications.
- NOAA Weather Radio and official weather alerts for severe conditions.
- Keep phone notifications enabled while traveling through hazard-prone areas.

## City Safety Habits

- Use licensed transport options late at night.
- Keep hotel and transport addresses saved offline.
- Avoid displaying valuables on crowded transit or nightlife corridors.

## Nature Safety Habits

- Start hikes early; carry water, layers, and navigation backup.
- Check heat, lightning, and flash-flood risk before departure.
- Respect trail closures and park warnings.

## Health and Air Quality

- Monitor AQI in wildfire smoke periods.
- Adjust outdoor plans when air quality is unhealthy.

## Incident Response Rule

If an itinerary segment becomes unsafe due to weather, smoke, flood, or wildfire warnings:
1. Stop movement
2. Rebase to a safer nearby city
3. Rebuild remaining days around open/low-risk activities
