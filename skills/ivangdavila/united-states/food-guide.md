# Food Guide — United States

## Regional Food Compass

| Region | Signature style | What to prioritize |
|--------|------------------|--------------------|
| Northeast | Delis, seafood, Italian-American depth | Neighborhood institutions over landmark strips |
| South | BBQ, soul food, Gulf seafood, Creole/Cajun | Regional specialties by city, not generic chains |
| Midwest | Steak, tavern culture, farm-to-table, deep comfort classics | Local icons + modern neighborhood spots |
| Southwest | Tex-Mex, New Mexican chile traditions, border-influenced cuisine | Heat-level and style distinctions by state |
| West Coast | Hyper-diverse immigrant food, produce-forward dining | Area-specific neighborhoods and market halls |
| Hawaii | Plate lunch, poke variants, island-specific local mixes | Local plate shops beyond resort zones |

## Restaurant Planning Rules

- Prime spots in NYC/LA/SF/Chicago often need advance reservations.
- In major tourist districts, moving a few streets can improve quality-price ratio.
- In park towns, dinner slots can sell out early; book before arrival day.

## Practical Dining Tips

- Ask for neighborhood-level options by cuisine and budget.
- Build one anchor dining reservation per day max.
- Protect schedule for jet lag and transfer delays before fixed reservations.
