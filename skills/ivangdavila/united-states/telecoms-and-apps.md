# Telecoms and Apps — United States

## Connectivity Setup

- Prefer eSIM or prepaid plans activated before arrival.
- Keep a second low-cost fallback data option for long road routes.
- Download offline maps for low-coverage national park corridors.

## Payments and Transit Apps

| Use case | Recommended app/system |
|----------|------------------------|
| NYC subway/bus | OMNY (tap card/phone) |
| Los Angeles transit | TAP |
| Bay Area rail/transit | Clipper + BART tools |
| National parks | NPS App + Recreation.gov |

## Travel Execution Apps

- Airline and rail official apps for real-time gate/platform changes
- Weather alert apps for storms and heat events
- Air quality tracking for wildfire season areas

## Battery and Device Hygiene

- Carry power bank and cable access in daypack.
- Keep screenshots of tickets, hotel addresses, and reservation numbers.
- Save key confirmations offline for poor-signal situations.

## Practical Rule

No phone = no itinerary. Build redundancy for maps, payment, and reservations.
