# Regions — United States

## Region Selection Grid

| Region | Best for | Watch-outs |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Northeast | First-time cities, museums, history, rail links | Winter storms, premium lodging costs |
| Southeast | Beaches, warm weather, theme parks, road trips | Summer heat/humidity, hurricane season |
| Midwest/Great Lakes | Architecture, food cities, lakefront summers | Harsh winter weather in many months |
| Southwest | Desert parks, geology, scenic drives | Extreme heat, long driving distances |
| Mountain West | Hiking, alpine scenery, parks | Altitude and weather volatility |
| West Coast | Coastal cities, food, national parks | High costs, wildfire/smoke periods |
| Pacific Northwest | Forest/coast/city blend | Rain planning and shoulder-season daylight |
| Hawaii | Beaches, volcanoes, island culture | Inter-island logistics, high costs |
| Alaska | Wildlife, wilderness, summer daylight | Expensive logistics, seasonal closures |

## Planning Rule of Thumb

- 7 days: 1 region
- 10-14 days: 1-2 regions
- 21 days: 2-3 regions with flights and explicit buffers

## Transfer Reality

- East Coast corridor enables more rail flexibility.
- Western routes often require flights or long drives.
- Park-heavy itineraries are usually car-dependent.

## First-Time U.S. Route Suggestions

- Culture + food: Northeast + Chicago
- Nature + road trip: Southwest + Mountain West
- Sun + family: Florida + selected East Coast city
- City + coast: California split-base model
