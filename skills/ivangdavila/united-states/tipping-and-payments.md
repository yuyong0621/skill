# Tipping and Payments — United States

## Card and Cash Reality

- Cards are dominant across major U.S. destinations.
- Keep one backup physical card and a small cash reserve for edge cases.

## Tipping Baseline (Tourism Context)

- Full-service restaurants: tipping is generally expected.
- Bars, rides, and hotel service roles usually involve smaller tip norms.
- If service charge is already included, adjust to avoid double tipping.

## Tax at Checkout

- Posted prices usually do not include sales tax.
- Final checkout totals vary by state/locality.
- Some states have no statewide sales tax, but local taxes may still apply.

## Payment Traps

- Dynamic currency conversion at payment terminal often worsens rates.
- Hotel "resort" or "destination" fees can be excluded from headline rates.
- Parking, toll, and baggage add-ons can shift daily cost materially.

## Practical Rule

Always price the real total (room + taxes + fees + transport + tips), not just base rates.
