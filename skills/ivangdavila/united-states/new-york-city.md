# New York City — Playbook

## Best Use Case

First-time U.S. city base with high museum density, strong public transit, and large food variety.

## Neighborhood Strategy

- Manhattan for first-time efficiency and late-night transit convenience.
- Brooklyn for food and neighborhood depth with commute tradeoff.
- Queens for value and airport convenience in selected zones.

## Movement Rules

- Use subway/bus tap payments (OMNY) for day-to-day movement.
- Cluster days by zone (Downtown, Midtown, Uptown, Brooklyn sectors).
- Avoid cross-city ping-pong between bookings.

## Timing Moves

- Major observatories and top museums: prebook timed entries.
- Broadway and headline experiences: reserve earlier in peak periods.
- Keep one rainy-day museum block and one clear-sky skyline block.

## Cost Traps

- Hotel location that looks cheaper but adds 60-90 min/day transit.
- Over-indexing on rideshare for every movement.
- High-markup tourist strips for every meal.

## 3-Day Skeleton

- Day 1: Lower Manhattan + waterfront + evening skyline
- Day 2: Midtown core + museum block + show/night plan
- Day 3: Brooklyn or Uptown + neighborhood food crawl
