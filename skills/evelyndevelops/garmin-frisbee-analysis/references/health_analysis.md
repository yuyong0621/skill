# Health Data Analysis Guide — Frisbee Edition

Science-backed framework for interpreting Garmin health metrics in the context of competitive Ultimate Frisbee. Use this when the player asks about their health, recovery, or performance trends.

---

## HRV (Heart Rate Variability) — Nightly Average

### What it means
HRV measures the variation in time between heartbeats, reflecting autonomic nervous system (ANS) balance. Higher HRV = more adaptable, resilient nervous system = better recovery capacity. For frisbee players, HRV is the best overnight indicator of whether you're recovered enough for intense play.

### Normal ranges (wrist-based wearable)
| Age | Low | Normal | High |
|-----|-----|--------|------|
| 20-29 | <25ms | 25-105ms | >105ms |
| 30-39 | <20ms | 20-80ms | >80ms |
| 40-49 | <15ms | 15-60ms | >60ms |
| 50-59 | <12ms | 12-45ms | >45ms |

*Note: Wrist-based HRV reads lower than chest strap. Personal baseline matters more than population norms.*

### Frisbee interpretation
- **Pre-tournament HRV**: Compare to your 7-day baseline. If HRV is ≥ baseline, you're recovered and ready.
- **HRV drop during tournament**: Expected — each game day will pull HRV lower. A drop >10% signals accumulated fatigue.
- **HRV not recovering between tournament nights**: Sign of cumulative fatigue; adjust game-day intensity.
- **Declining HRV across a training block** (3+ days): back off intensity, add rest day before next game.
- **Acute drop**: poor sleep, alcohol, travel stress, intense previous day, illness onset.

### Key research findings
- Low HRV is associated with increased injury risk in team sport athletes
- HRV reflects both physical AND psychological stress (pre-game nerves show up as lower HRV)
- 7-day rolling average provides more meaningful context than daily values

## Body Battery (0-100) — Pre-Game Readiness

### What it means
Garmin's proprietary recovery score based on HRV, stress, sleep quality, and activity intensity. For frisbee players, the **pre-game Body Battery level is the single most actionable readiness indicator**.

### Frisbee readiness zones
| Level | Range | Frisbee Meaning |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| Ready | 75-100 | Full output — push hard, sprint freely |
| Manageable | 50-74 | Normal performance, monitor fatigue |
| Compromised | 25-49 | Conserve energy, prioritize smart play over sprinting |
| Depleted | 0-24 | Performance significantly limited — prioritize recovery |

### Tournament patterns to watch
- **Pre-game 1 Body Battery**: Should ideally be ≥75. If <50, you're starting the tournament already fatigued.
- **Body Battery between games**: How fast you recharge during rest. Faster = better recovery.
- **Day-over-day tournament pattern**: Expect declining peaks. Day 1 should be highest; Day 3 lowest.
- **Not recovering above 50 overnight**: Critical under-recovery — sleep quality or load is unsustainable.
- **Consistently <50 peak across tournament**: Consider reducing game load or prioritizing sleep.

### Optimal tournament pattern
- Arrive at tournament with BB ≥ 75
- End each game day with BB no lower than 20-30
- Recharge to ≥ 60-70 overnight (hard with tournament sleep quality)
- Pre-game 2+ starting at 50+ is still manageable

## Resting Heart Rate (RHR)

### Normal ranges
| Fitness level | RHR (bpm) |
|--------------|-----------|
| Athlete | 40-55 |
| Active adult | 55-65 |
| Average adult | 60-80 |

### Frisbee interpretation
- **RHR elevated 3-5+ bpm above baseline**: accumulated fatigue, dehydration, or illness onset — monitor closely before playing intensely
- **RHR rising across tournament days**: expected; combined with HRV drop signals cumulative fatigue
- **Post-tournament RHR returning to baseline within 48-72h**: sign of good recovery capacity
- **Sustained elevated RHR (5+ days)**: overtraining or illness — reduce load

## Sleep Analysis — Tournament Recovery

### Optimal distribution (for 7-8h total sleep)
| Stage | % of total | Ideal (8h) | Function |
|-------|-----------|------------|----------|
| Deep | 15-25% | 1.2-2.0h | Physical restoration, muscle repair, growth hormone |
| REM | 20-25% | 1.6-2.0h | Emotional processing, motor learning, memory |
| Light | 50-60% | 4.0-4.8h | Transition stage |
| Awake | <10% | <0.8h | Normal brief awakenings |

### Sleep Score (0-100)
| Score | Quality | Frisbee Implication |
|-------|---------|---------------------|
| 90-100 | Excellent | Optimal recovery; peak performance likely |
| 80-89 | Good | Quality recovery; normal performance expected |
| 60-79 | Fair | Adequate; conserve energy early in day |
| 0-59 | Poor | Under-recovered; manage intensity carefully |

### Frisbee-specific sleep notes
- **Night before tournament**: 8+ hours is ideal; deep sleep is especially important for leg muscle recovery
- **Tournament nights**: Sleep in unfamiliar places (hotels, tents) commonly reduces deep sleep — accept the limitation
- **Deep sleep <1h**: reduced muscle repair — expect more soreness between tournament days
- **REM <1.2h**: may reduce reaction time and game sense
- **Consistent sleep score <70 during training block**: recovery is insufficient for training load — risk of overuse injury

### Factors relevant to frisbee players
**Reduces sleep quality**: alcohol post-game, late dinners, loud/bright hotel environments, high caffeine day-of
**Improves sleep quality**: cool room (60-67°F), consistent bedtime, light stretching post-game, no screens 30min before bed

## Stress Levels

### Ranges
| Level | Score | Interpretation |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| Rest | 0-25 | Recovering — ideal between games |
| Low | 26-50 | Calm, ready for moderate output |
| Medium | 51-75 | Active or elevated — game-level exertion |
| High | 76-100 | Intense activity, pre-game nerves, or emotional stress |

### Frisbee interpretation
- **Between-game stress**: Should drop to Low (26-50) during rest periods. If staying High, you're not recovering between games.
- **Pre-game stress spikes without activity**: pre-competition nerves — normal, but affects Body Battery drain
- **All-day stress average on non-game days**: Should be <50 to support training adaptation
- **No rest periods during tournament day**: concerning — consider active recovery, breathing exercises between games

## Heart Rate Zones — Frisbee Context

### Zones (based on max HR %)
| Zone | % Max HR | Frisbee Meaning |
|------|----------|----------------|
| Zone 1 | 50-60% | Walking, very light recovery |
| Zone 2 | 60-70% | Jogging, between-point movement |
| Zone 3 | 70-80% | Sustained running, easy flow plays |
| Zone 4 | 80-90% | Hard cuts, sustained high-intensity defense |
| Zone 5 | 90-95% | Sprint cuts, layout bids, full-field sprints |
| Zone 6 | 95-100% | Maximum sprint, short explosive bursts |

### What to look for in a frisbee game
- **High Zone 4-6 time (>30% of game)**: High-intensity game — expect more recovery time needed
- **Zone 4-6 time declining across tournament**: Game-day fatigue reducing ability to reach high intensities
- **Low Zone 4-6 in training (<15%)**: Training may not be game-specific enough — add more sprint intervals
- **Avg HR per game increasing across tournament days**: Working harder for same output = cumulative fatigue

## Sprint Fatigue Index

### What it means
Ratio of last 3 sprint peak speeds to first 3 sprint peak speeds within a single game.

| Value | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| ≥ 0.95 | Stable sprint output — maintained power through the game |
| 0.85-0.94 | Mild decline — some speed loss, still functional |
| < 0.85 | Significant fatigue — later sprints meaningfully slower |

### Frisbee interpretation
- **SFI < 0.85 in game 1 of a tournament**: poor pre-tournament preparation or insufficient warm-up
- **SFI declining across tournament games**: cumulative fatigue — each game day leaves you with less sprint capacity
- **SFI consistently < 0.85 in training**: may indicate inadequate training fitness or insufficient rest between sessions

## Heart Rate Recovery (HRR) — Post-Game

### What it means
How fast your HR drops after a game ends. Steeper drop = better cardiovascular fitness and recovery.

### Reference thresholds
| Drop in first 60 seconds | Fitness level |
|--------------------------|--------------|
| >25 bpm | Good cardiovascular recovery |
| 15-25 bpm | Average |
| <15 bpm | Below average — consider aerobic base training |

### Frisbee interpretation
- **Compare HRR curves across tournament games**: Flatter curves in later games = cumulative fatigue
- **HRR slope getting shallower over a season**: aerobic fitness may be declining — increase aerobic base work
- **Very slow HRR (drop <10 bpm in 60s)**: possible dehydration, heat stress, illness, or low fitness

## VO2 Max

### What it means
Maximum oxygen uptake (ml/kg/min). Gold standard for aerobic capacity — directly relevant to frisbee endurance across a full tournament.

### Ranges (general population)
#### Women
| Age | Poor | Fair | Good | Excellent | Superior |
|-----|------|------|------|-----------|----------|
| 20-29 | <32 | 32-36 | 37-41 | 42-46 | >46 |
| 30-39 | <30 | 30-33 | 34-39 | 40-44 | >44 |

#### Men
| Age | Poor | Fair | Good | Excellent | Superior |
|-----|------|------|------|-----------|----------|
| 20-29 | <40 | 40-43 | 44-51 | 52-56 | >56 |
| 30-39 | <38 | 38-41 | 42-49 | 50-54 | >54 |

### Frisbee context
- Frisbee is an intermittent high-intensity sport — VO2 max >45 (women) / >50 (men) supports full-tournament performance without major aerobic limitation
- VO2 max improves with consistent aerobic base training (Zone 2 running) and interval work
- Garmin estimates from outdoor GPS + HR activities — ensure some runs have HR data for accurate tracking

## Analysis Framework for Frisbee Players

When answering "how am I doing?" or "am I ready to play?":

### 1. Pre-Game Readiness Check
- Body Battery level right now vs pre-game target (≥75 = ready, ≥50 = manageable)
- Last night's sleep score + hours + deep sleep
- Morning HRV vs personal 7-day baseline
- Resting HR vs personal baseline

### 2. Post-Game Analysis
- Sprint count, top speed, Sprint Fatigue Index
- Time in Zone 4-6 (intensity distribution)
- Heart Rate Recovery curve slope
- Body Battery drain rate during game

### 3. Tournament Trend Analysis
- Body Battery: day-over-day peak values (expect decline; how steep is it?)
- HRV each morning: tracking cumulative fatigue
- HRR curves: getting flatter = more fatigued
- Sprint Fatigue Index: declining across games = reduced power output

### 4. Season-Level Trends (30-90 day window)
- VO2 max trend (rising = fitness improving)
- HRV baseline trend (rising = adaptation, falling = overtraining)
- Top sprint speed across games
- Sleep score average during heavy training periods vs taper

### 5. Flags / Alerts
- **Body Battery <30 pre-game**: performance likely compromised — communicate this to coaching
- **HRV >15% below baseline 2+ days**: accumulated fatigue — reduce training load
- **RHR elevated 5+ bpm for 3+ days**: illness/overtraining watch — monitor closely
- **Sprint Fatigue Index <0.85 in multiple consecutive games**: inadequate inter-game recovery
- **Sleep <6h average for 3+ tournament nights**: serious sleep debt — prioritize earlier bedtime
- **HRR slope getting flatter across tournament days**: cumulative cardiovascular fatigue

## Important Disclaimers
- This is NOT medical advice — recommend consulting a doctor for health concerns
- Wearable data has accuracy limitations (wrist HR ±5-10 bpm during intense movement)
- Individual baselines matter more than population norms
- One bad day doesn't indicate a problem — look for 3+ day patterns
- Context matters: travel, heat, altitude, illness, stress all affect metrics
- Sprint detection uses GPS speed — accuracy depends on GPS lock quality
