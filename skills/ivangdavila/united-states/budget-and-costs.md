# Budget and Costs — United States

## Useful Budget Bands (Per Person / Day)

| Style | Typical range | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Budget | USD 90-180 | Hostels/budget hotels, transit focus, selective paid attractions |
| Mid-range | USD 180-350 | 3-star hotels, mixed transport, paid attractions + dining |
| Premium | USD 350+ | Prime-location hotels, frequent flights/rideshare, top dining |

## Major Cost Drivers

- Region and season (NYC/SF/Hawaii/Alaska can spike strongly)
- Last-minute flights and park-adjacent lodging
- Car rental + parking + toll bundles
- Attraction/park permits in peak windows

## Cost Control Levers

- Fewer bases, longer stays per base
- Shoulder-season timing where weather still works
- Early booking for parks, islands, and event-heavy cities
- Transit-first plans in major city cores

## Booking Order for Cost + Risk Control

1. Entry/admin constraints confirmed
2. High-risk inventory (park permits, key flights)
3. Lodging near primary daily movement
4. Secondary activities and dining

## Hidden Costs Checklist

- Hotel taxes and nightly fees
- Baggage and seat fees
- Airport transfer costs
- Attraction service/booking fees
- Data roaming fallback costs

## Planning Rule

If total trip budget is tight, reduce region count first. Do not cut every day to minimum comfort.
